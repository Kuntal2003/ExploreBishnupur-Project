Why Bishnupur Stands Out?

This section highlights what makes Bishnupur special - from its timeless terracotta temples to its silk sarees and musical legacy. A must-visit town in Bankura, West Bengal.

Ma Mrinmoyee Temple
Established in 997 CE by the Mallas, oldest Durga temple, hosting ancient Durga Puja traditions.
View Location on Google Maps
Rasmancha
Built in 1600 AD, Rasmancha housed Rash festival idols; features unique mixed architecture, ASI protected.
View Location on Google Maps
Lalbandh
Built by Raghunath Singha for Lalbai, Lalbandh blends legend, nature, eco-tourism, and cultural significance.
View Location on Google Maps
Joypur Forest
Dense forest near Bishnupur with rich biodiversity, scenic trails, wildlife, eco-resorts, and historical significance.
View Location on Google Maps
Bishnupur Aerodrome
Built in WWII, used by U.S. Army Air Force; hosted B-24, F-5, B-29 aircraft operations.
View Location on Google Maps
Bishnupur Music Festival
Celebrates Bishnupur’s musical heritage, honoring ancient pioneers through West Bengal Tourism’s cultural initiative.
View Location on Google Maps
